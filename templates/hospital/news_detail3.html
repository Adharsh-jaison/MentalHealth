The neglected and disregarded issue in India: Mental Health problem in students

Indian students’ mental health issues are a quiet catastrophe that can go unreported despite the country’s reputation for academic brilliance and a rich cultural legacy. The emotional well-being of pupils is frequently overlooked and disregarded in India’s educational system, which places a strong emphasis on academic success. To highlight the urgency of this situation and throw light on it, this post seeks to do just that.

There are varied reasons why the number of students dealing with mental health issues has significantly increased in recent years. Students’ mental health is negatively impacted by the atmosphere that has been created by the pressure to perform well academically and the high expectations of society and parents. The situation is made worse by the fierce rivalry for a few possibilities, which makes students stressed, anxious, and depressed.

The difficulty is exacerbated by the lack of knowledge and understanding of mental health concerns. Due to the stigma surrounding mental health issues, many students endure their suffering in secret and are reluctant to seek treatment. Because of this, their difficulties frequently go unreported, which has long-term effects on their general well-being.

As a result, it is having untreated mental health issues in students and can have serious consequences. Students who struggle to concentrate and manage the demands of their academics may fare worse academically. Their mental health may deteriorate further as a result of the stress and underperformance that might result from this condition.
