How to cope with your mental health as an international student

Back in 2015, a study found that around 25% of undergraduate students in the US had dealt with a mental health condition within the previous year (American College Health Association, 2015, p. 15). And mind you, that number only includes students who actually sought professional help or got diagnosed. If we consider those who didn't seek any treatment, the percentage is likely to be even higher. Can you imagine? More than half of the students, about 57%, reported overwhelming anxiety, while 34% struggled so much with depression that it affected their daily functioning (p. 14). It's safe to say that mental health is a significant concern on college campuses across the globe.

Now, let's shift our focus to students studying abroad. You'd think that the percentage of students facing mental health challenges while abroad would be similar, if not higher, compared to those on domestic campuses. After all, factors like culture shock, homesickness, and stress can trigger anxiety, depression, or other mental health issues, especially for students who have already dealt with such concerns.

Studying abroad can be a life-changing experience for Indian students, but it can also bring forth unique challenges to their mental well-being. By implementing coping strategies and accessing available resources, Indian students can navigate these challenges and prioritise their mental health. From establishing a support network to engaging in self-care practices, maintaining a routine, and seeking professional help, when necessary, there are various ways to cope with mental issues while studying abroad.
